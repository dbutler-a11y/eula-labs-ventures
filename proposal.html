The Thesis (From Our Published Research)

The Claw Protocol Collective published a peer-reviewable research paper: "Coordinated Intelligence Systems: A Community-Led, Agent-Native Framework for Accelerated Technological Iteration, Human Advancement, and Sustainable Infrastructure."

The core economic model (Section 7 of the paper) operates on a dual-track structure:

TrackPurposeRevenue Model

Managed ServicesCommercial products built on open-source foundationsSubscriptions, licensing, consulting, hardware sales

This means 65% of all revenue goes directly back to contributors and infrastructure — not extracted by VCs or corporate interests. A financial steward ensures these allocations are tracked, compliant, and auditable.

Fund Steward Options

You don't need to be the compliance layer yourself. You need to facilitate and manage the relationship with an all-in-one fund stewardship entity that handles: investor capital management, token tracking, distributions, attorney filings, and regulatory compliance.

Here are three options, each with different tradeoffs:

Option A — Recommended

SPV Platform (Allocations, AngelList Stack, or Rally)

A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) is a single-purpose legal entity created specifically for this investment. Platforms like Allocations.com handle everything: entity formation, investor onboarding, capital collection, distributions, K-1 tax docs, and compliance — all in one dashboard.

Entity FormationLLC (Delaware or Texas) created automatically
Investor OnboardingAccredited investor verification, subscription docs, wire instructions
Capital ManagementDedicated bank account, capital call tracking
DistributionsAutomated pro-rata distributions when revenue flows
Tax & LegalK-1 generation, annual filings, registered agent
Attorney FilingsOperating agreement, PPM (Private Placement Memorandum) if needed
Setup Cost$5,000 – $10,000 (one-time) + ~$2K/year admin
Timeline2-3 weeks to fully operational

Pros

  • Turnkey — one platform handles everything
  • Familiar to PE/M&A professionals
  • Investor-grade compliance out of the box
  • Automated distributions and tax docs
  • Can add investors later without restructuring

Cons

  • Platform fees reduce deployed capital
  • Doesn't natively handle contribution tokens
  • May feel heavy for a $50K-$100K raise

Why this is recommended: At your M&A/PE background, this is the structure you already know. It gives us institutional-grade compliance from day one while being fast to set up. The token/contribution scoring layer runs separately through our Discord bot and open-source tracking system — the SPV just handles the money.

Option B — Most Control

Texas LLC + Securities Attorney + Fund Admin Software

Form a Texas LLC directly with a securities attorney who drafts a custom operating agreement encoding the contribution scoring system, revenue allocation, and governance structure from the research paper. Pair with cap table software (Carta or Pulley) and a CPA.

EntityTexas LLC (Series LLC option for multi-pillar isolation)
AttorneySecurities attorney drafts operating agreement + reviews token structure
Cap TableCarta or Pulley for equity/token tracking
AccountingQuickBooks + CPA for quarterly reporting and distributions
Token ComplianceAttorney reviews contribution tokens against SEC utility token guidance
Setup Cost$8,000 – $15,000 (attorney $5K-$8K + software + CPA)
Timeline4-6 weeks

Pros

  • Full custom control over every term
  • Operating agreement encodes the 5-tier scoring system
  • Series LLC can isolate each pillar (Properties, ARIA, Protocol)
  • Attorney ensures token structure is SEC-compliant
  • Most defensible long-term

Cons

  • Slower setup (attorney drafting takes time)
  • Higher upfront cost
  • More moving parts to manage
  • Need to coordinate multiple vendors

Best if: We plan to raise beyond $100K or want to bring in additional investors in the near term. The Series LLC structure lets each pillar (Properties, ARIA, Protocol) operate as a separate liability-protected series under one umbrella — clean for investors, clean for accounting.

Option C — Most Aligned with Research Paper

Open Collective Fiscal Sponsor + Lightweight LLC

Open Collective provides fiscal hosting for open-source projects: transparent budgets, open expense reporting, and a legal entity that holds funds on behalf of the community. Pair with a lightweight LLC for the commercial/managed services arm.

Community TreasuryOpen Collective fiscal sponsor (transparent, public budget)
Commercial EntityLightweight Texas LLC for managed services revenue
Token TrackingClaw Protocol Discord bot + GitHub contribution logs
LegalStartup attorney for operating agreement ($2K-$3K)
ComplianceOpen Collective handles 501(c)(3) or fiscal sponsor compliance
Setup Cost$3,000 – $6,000 (LLC + attorney + Open Collective fees ~5%)
Timeline1-2 weeks

Pros

  • Lowest setup cost
  • Radically transparent (public budget aligns with paper Section 10.4)
  • Fast to launch
  • Community-native — contributors can see every dollar
  • Open Collective is trusted by major open-source projects

Cons

  • 5% fiscal sponsor fee on all transactions
  • Less familiar to PE/M&A investors
  • Doesn't handle accredited investor verification
  • LLC side still needs separate accounting

Best if: We prioritize speed and transparency over institutional structure. This is the cheapest path to get capital deployed while maintaining the open, auditable ethos of the research paper. Can always upgrade to Option A or B later as we scale.

My recommendation: Start with Option A (SPV platform) for the initial $50K-$100K investment. It's the fastest path to deployed capital with professional compliance. As revenue grows and we bring in more investors, we can layer on Option B (Series LLC) for long-term structure. Your role is to evaluate these platforms, select one, and manage the relationship — not to do the compliance work yourself.

Profit Center Roadmap

The research paper (Section 7) defines the dual-track model. Here are the specific profit centers we build first, ordered by speed to revenue:

Weeks 1-4 — Immediate Revenue

1. Agent-as-a-Service (OpenClaw)

Deploy AI agents that perform real work: web scraping, content generation, lead qualification, data processing. Clients pay per task or monthly subscription. We already have the Agent Workstation infrastructure running on DigitalOcean — this is about spawning agents at scale and selling their output.

Revenue target: $2K-$5K/month within 60 days

Weeks 2-6 — Hardware Revenue

2. ARIA Device Pre-Orders & Kit Sales

Assembled ARIA companion robots sold as kits or pre-built units. Four models from $220-$430. The research and open-source plans are done — we need to build physical units, test them, and start taking orders. Grant applications (NIH SBIR, NSF I-Corps) submitted in parallel for non-dilutive funding.

Revenue target: $5K-$15K from first batch + grant applications in pipeline

Weeks 4-8 — Service Revenue

3. AI Consulting & Implementation (EULA Labs)

Enterprise AI consulting leveraging the agent frameworks we've built. Help businesses deploy AI agents, automate workflows, integrate LLMs. The EULA Labs brand already exists — this is about activating it as a revenue line with the infrastructure we now have.

Revenue target: $5K-$10K/month from 2-3 retainer clients

Months 2-4 — Platform Revenue

4. Managed Compute & GPU-as-a-Service

Once we own compute hardware, we sell excess capacity. Community members and external users rent GPU time for inference, fine-tuning, and agent hosting. This is the compute sovereignty play from the paper (Section 9) — but it also generates revenue from day one.

Revenue target: $3K-$8K/month (scales with hardware investment)

Months 3-6 — Real Estate Revenue

5. Pembrook Compound Activation

The 3-unit Pembrook compound generates revenue through: co-working space rental for the AI training campus, short-term furnished rentals on accessory units, and hosting for community events/workshops. Real estate revenue provides the stable base layer that de-risks everything above.

Revenue target: $3K-$6K/month from rental income

Combined Year 1 revenue target: $150K – $400K across all five profit centers. Conservative estimates assume only 2-3 centers activate successfully. The key insight: we're not building one thing and hoping it works. We're activating multiple revenue streams simultaneously, and the infrastructure (agents, compute, real estate) compounds across all of them.
